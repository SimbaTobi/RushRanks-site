Child Sexual Abuse & Exploitation (CSAE) Policy

Last updated: October 12, 2025

Our stance

RushRanks has a zero-tolerance policy toward any content that sexually exploits or endangers children. We remove such content, permanently disable accounts, and report apparent child sexual abuse material (CSAM) to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) and/or law enforcement as required by U.S. law.

Scope & definitions

  • Child: any person under 18 years of age.
  • CSAM: imagery, video, audio, text, or other content that depicts, promotes, solicits, or facilitates sexual abuse or exploitation of a child, including grooming, sexualized nudity of minors, or sexual contact.
  • Sexual exploitation: any attempt to coerce, entice, trade, monetize, or otherwise exploit a minor for sexual purposes (including grooming and sextortion).

Prohibited content & conduct

The following are strictly prohibited and will result in immediate removal and account action:

  • Any CSAM or sexualized depictions of minors (real or edited/synthetic).
  • Grooming, sexual solicitation of minors, or attempts to obtain sexual images from minors.
  • Instructions or offers to trade CSAM; links to CSAM; or attempts to evade detection.
  • Sexualized commentary about minors or any content that sexualizes a child’s body.

Detection & moderation

We use a combination of user reports, automated tools, and human review to detect potential violations. Automated tools (including AWS Rekognition visual moderation) assist with triage. Final enforcement decisions are made by trained human reviewers. Automated signals are not treated as proof on their own.

Reporting content to us

If you encounter suspected CSAM or grooming on RushRanks:

  1. Use the in-app Report button on the video or profile, and select the most accurate reason.
  2. Or email our Safety team: support@rushranks.com with links, timestamps, and any relevant details. Do not download or redistribute the content.

We prioritize CSAE reports and will take appropriate action, which may include removal, account termination, preservation of evidence, and reporting to NCMEC/law enforcement.

Our legal obligations

  • As a U.S. service provider, when we obtain actual knowledge of apparent CSAM on our service, we will make reports to NCMEC CyberTipline pursuant to 18 U.S.C. §2258A.
  • We cooperate with lawful requests from law enforcement, consistent with applicable law and our Privacy Policy.
  • We preserve relevant data for investigations and restrict access to the smallest number of trained personnel necessary.

Account actions

We may remove content, restrict features, suspend, or permanently disable accounts associated with CSAE. We may also block associated devices, emails, and other identifiers to prevent re-offending. Appeals may be available for non-CSAE enforcement; CSAE decisions are generally final given safety and legal obligations.

Minors on RushRanks

RushRanks is available only to users aged 13+ in the United States. We prohibit any sexual content involving minors. Parents/guardians and schools are encouraged to discuss online safety with children and teens.

Contact

Safety & reporting: support@rushranks.com

For emergencies or imminent harm, contact local law enforcement immediately before notifying RushRanks.

This CSAE Policy is part of our Terms and references our Privacy Policy. Nothing here limits our ability to remove content or refer matters to authorities for the protection of children and the community.
